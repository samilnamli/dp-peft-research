\section{Experimental Setup}

\paragraph{Models and tasks.}
We evaluate two transformer models under six differential privacy placement
strategies combined with two parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{DistilBERT-base-uncased} on SST-2 binary sentiment
        (67k / 872 train/test), adapter PEFT at $\eps \in \{0.5,1,2,4,8\}$
        and LoRA PEFT at $\eps \in \{1,8\}$.
  \item \textbf{BERT-base-uncased} on AG News 4-class classification
        (120k / 7.6k), adapter PEFT at $\eps \in \{1,8\}$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{DP placement strategies.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{No DP} --- standard PEFT fine-tuning, upper-bound baseline.
  \item \textbf{Adapter-Only DP} --- DP-SGD on adapter modules only;
        backbone and head frozen / non-private.
  \item \textbf{Head+Adapter DP} --- DP-SGD on adapters + classification head.
  \item \textbf{Partial Backbone DP} --- DP-SGD on adapters + top-2 transformer blocks.
  \item \textbf{Last-Layer DP} --- DP-SGD on the final classification head only.
  \item \textbf{Full-Model DP} --- DP-SGD on all trainable parameters.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Hyperparameters.}
Batch size 64, learning rate $5 \times 10^{-4}$, 20 epochs, seed 42.
Privacy budget matched at the stated $(\eps, \delta)$ using Opacus
\texttt{get\_noise\_multiplier}.

\paragraph{Empirical privacy evaluation.}
After training, a threshold membership inference attack (MIA) is run
on each checkpoint.
Attack AUC $= 0.5$ and Advantage $= 0$ indicate perfect empirical privacy.

The larger AG News dataset (120k vs.\ 67k) and 4-class task diversity prevent
the per-sample gradient signal from becoming a reliable membership indicator,
regardless of placement strategy.

\paragraph{Cross-model MIA finding.}
BERT maintains near-perfect empirical privacy ($0.500$--$0.527$) for \emph{all}
placements, while DistilBERT Full-Model DP AUC ($0.601$) \emph{exceeds} the
no-DP baseline ($0.597$).
This counter-intuitive result for DistilBERT arises because aggressive noise
degrades generalisation while memorisation of specific examples persists,
creating a stronger loss-based MIA signal.
BERT avoids this due to the larger AG News dataset and richer 4-class task.

\section{Summary of Findings}

\begin{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{PEFT-based DP placements consistently outperform Full-Model DP.}
    At $\eps=1$, the best PEFT placement (Adapter-Only) outperforms Full-Model DP by
    $\approx 8$ pp on SST-2 (DistilBERT) and $\approx 7$ pp on AG News (BERT),
    while consuming the same formal privacy budget.

  \item \textbf{Formal $\eps$ and empirical MIA leakage are decoupled.}
    Tightening $\eps$ from 8 to 0.5 reduces MIA AUC by at most 0.021 for
    any placement, despite a 2.2--4.0 pp accuracy cost.
    Full-Model DP is the only strategy where MIA leakage \emph{increases} with $\eps$.

  \item \textbf{Last-Layer DP achieves the best empirical privacy,
    below random-chance MIA at every $\eps$.}
    MIA AUC $\in [0.493, 0.498]$ across all $\eps$ on SST-2 ---
    the only strategy to consistently outperform the $0.50$ random baseline.

  \item \textbf{Full-Model DP is the worst choice on all axes.}
    It delivers the lowest accuracy ($-10$--$14$ pp), the highest MIA leakage
    (AUC up to $0.601$, exceeding no-DP), and is $4\times$ slower per epoch.

  \item \textbf{Head+Adapter DP and Partial Backbone DP offer the best overall tradeoff.}
    Within 4--5 pp of no-DP accuracy, lower MIA leakage than Adapter-Only,
    minimal $\eps$-sensitivity, and reasonable efficiency.

  \item \textbf{BERT achieves near-perfect empirical privacy across all placements.}
    MIA AUC $\in [0.500, 0.527]$ for BERT regardless of placement or $\eps$.
    Larger dataset (120k vs.\ 67k) and richer 4-class task prevent
    reliable membership inference even without tight DP noise.

  \item \textbf{BERT Full-Model DP corrected: same universal cost, unique privacy profile.}
    Corrected BERT Full-Model DP drops $-9.4$ pp at $\eps=8$ and $-12.4$ pp at $\eps=1$,
    matching DistilBERT's accuracy cost pattern.
    Yet BERT MIA AUC remains $\approx 0.502$ --- empirical privacy holds
    even when formal noise is maximally destructive.

  \item \textbf{LoRA marginally outperforms Adapter in accuracy; both behave similarly on MIA.}
    Mean accuracy advantage of LoRA: $+0.3$ pp at $\eps=8$, $+0.2$ pp at $\eps=1$.
    MIA AUC difference: $< 0.01$ for all placements.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Experimental Notes}

\paragraph{Opacus compatibility.}
All models use \texttt{ModuleValidator.fix()} to replace \texttt{LayerNorm} with
\texttt{GroupNorm} for Opacus compatibility.

\paragraph{BERT Full-Model DP implementation history.}
The initial BERT Full-Model DP runs (Apr 17, 2026) produced results identical to
Head+Adapter DP.
Root cause: adapter PEFT freezes the backbone (\texttt{requires\_grad=False}),
so Opacus only wrapped and noised the trainable head+adapter parameters ---
the same set as Head+Adapter DP.
A re-run attempt (Apr 18) crashed with an Opacus \texttt{RuntimeError} (tensor size
mismatch in per-sample gradient stacking when frozen and trainable layers are mixed).
Corrected runs with unfrozen backbone completed Apr 18--23, 2026.
Results used throughout this document reflect the corrected implementation.

\paragraph{Reproducibility.}
All experiments use seed 42.
Full configurations, checkpoints, and result JSONs are tracked in the project
repository.
